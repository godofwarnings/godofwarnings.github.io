I'm a final-year student at IIIT Hyderabad, doing an integrated B.Tech in Computer Science and an M.S. by Research in Computational Natural Sciences. My time here has been split between research, engineering internships, and competitive hackathons.

On the research side, I work at CCNSB under Prof. Subhadip Mitra, building foundation models for particle jets at the LHC using self-supervised learning (JEPA). The goal is to get these models to generalise across downstream tasks using CERN open data. One paper is already out — accepted at the ICLR 2025 Workshop. Right now I'm working on the evaluation framework and digging into what these models are actually learning.

At CHUBB, I built a CI/CD-ready hybrid test automation framework with Playwright and a zero-code, YAML-driven test engine that cut scenario creation time by 90% — powered by GenAI and MCP tools. Before that at CIE, IIIT-H, I built real-time drone tracking maps with OpenLayers and a telemetry backend over MQTT and MAVLink, hitting sub-100ms latency across 5+ concurrent feeds.

On the side, I've built a distributed network file system in C with concurrent ops and fault tolerance, a custom Linux shell from scratch, a full-stack Reddit clone with Docker and nginx, and an AI-powered mental health journaling app running four models in parallel. Most of these exist because something about the problem was just too interesting to leave alone.

When I'm not doing any of that, you'll find me with a guitar, deep in a book or anime rabbit hole, or overthinking a chess position (I'm kidding mate… I don’t even know the rules.). I follow curiosity wherever it leads — which has taken me to some interesting places indeed.
